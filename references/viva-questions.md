# Viva / Defense Questions

35+ questions compiled from University of Calgary, Oxford, and Edinburgh examiner guides.

## Universal (expect ALL of these)

1. Can you summarise your thesis in 3 minutes?
2. What is the original contribution of this work?
3. What is the weakest part of your thesis?
4. What would you do differently if you started again?
5. How does your work fit into the broader field?
6. Why should anyone care about your results?
7. What are the practical implications?
8. How generalisable are your findings?

## Technical

9. Why did you choose this method over alternatives?
10. What are the assumptions of your model? Are they valid?
11. How did you handle missing data?
12. Is your sample size sufficient? How do you know?
13. Could your results be explained by a confounding variable?
14. What happens if you change parameter X?
15. Show me where in your thesis you address limitation Y.
16. How do you know your model is not overfitting?
17. Why this threshold and not another?
18. Explain your validation strategy.

## Challenging

19. Your results contradict Study X. How do you explain that?
20. This could be explained by a simpler mechanism. Convince me otherwise.
21. If I gave you two more years, what would you do?
22. Which of your findings surprised you the most?
23. What is the one figure that best tells the story of your thesis?
24. How confident are you in result X?
25. What ethical considerations did you face?
26. How would climate change affect the validity of your model?
27. Why should a forecaster trust your model over their own judgement?

## Difficult (prepare for these specifically)

28. Why didn't you do X instead? It seems more obvious.
29. Your literature review doesn't mention [important paper]. Why?
30. I don't see how Section X connects to your research question.
31. Isn't this just [oversimplification of your work]?
32. Other people have done similar work. What is actually new here?
33. Your error bars overlap. How can you claim significance?
34. This methodology section reads like a textbook. What did YOU actually decide?
35. If I removed Chapter X, would the thesis still stand?

## Defense Tips

- **Know your thesis cold.** Page numbers, figure numbers, exact values. "As shown in Table 4.3 on page 67..." impresses examiners.
- **Prepare a 5-minute summary.** Problem → gap → method → key result → significance.
- **Anticipate the weakest points.** Examiners will find them. Have answers ready.
- **"I don't know" is acceptable.** Follow with a hypothesis: "I have not investigated that, but based on the SHAP results, I would expect..."
- **Bring a printed copy** with tabs on key sections for quick reference.
- **Don't argue.** Listen, acknowledge, then explain. Examiners test your ability to engage, not to win.
